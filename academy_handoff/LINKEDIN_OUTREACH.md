# LINKEDIN_OUTREACH.md — Templates, Cadence, and DSAG Strategy

Your LinkedIn outreach plan for Months 2-6. Built around the DSAG/Hesse advantage.

---

## Strategy Summary

Three audience tiers, three message archs:

1. **SAP practice leads at Tier 1 SIs** — pitch Academy as SI onboarding tool, Studio as delivery accelerator, white-label as upside
2. **Heads of Data Platform at S/4HANA or ECC customers** — pitch Academy as team upskilling, Studio as extraction automation
3. **Microsoft/Snowflake/Databricks SAP partner leads** — pitch co-sell partnership, marketplace listing

DSAG and the German SAP ecosystem get priority treatment because of your Kelsterbach/Hesse location.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4 before outreach)

Before sending any outreach:

1. **Profile audit.** Your LinkedIn profile should clearly signal "SAP data extraction expert building a free training platform." Update:
   - Headline: "Building SAP Extract Academy — free walkthroughs for S/4HANA and ECC data extraction · SAP SME"
   - About section: 2-3 paragraphs on your SAP background + what the Academy is + what you're trying to accomplish
   - Featured section: Academy landing page URL + the ACDOCA deep-dive article

2. **Content cadence.** Start posting on LinkedIn 2-3×/week for 3-4 weeks BEFORE outreach. Builds credibility so cold messages feel less cold. Topics:
   - "I just published a walkthrough for extracting VBAK to ADLS"
   - "Common mistake: using SELECT * on ACDOCA"
   - "DSAG members: what's the #1 SAP extraction pain you see?"
   - Short observations on SAP Notes or CDS view releases

3. **DSAG membership.** Join DSAG if you haven't. Attend at least one Arbeitskreis (working group) meeting. The "Analytics und Big Data" or "Data Warehousing" working groups are natural fits. Introduce the Academy there first.

## Cadence

### Week-by-Week Outreach Plan

**Month 2 (after Academy MVP is live):**

- Week 5: Send 30 SI practice lead connection requests
- Week 6: Send 50 end-customer Head of Data connection requests. Follow up on SI connections accepted in week 5.
- Week 7: Send 20 cloud vendor partner requests. Follow up with non-responders from week 5-6.
- Week 8: Book discovery calls with anyone who responded positively.

**Month 3:**

- Week 9-10: Continue outreach to new targets. Begin DSAG-specific campaign (30 connections, focused on Hesse/Frankfurt members).
- Week 11: Present at a small DSAG meetup if possible.
- Week 12: Pilot proposals to 3 anchor customers.

**Daily limits:**

- 15-20 connection requests per day maximum (LinkedIn's practical throttle)
- 5-10 first messages per day (higher quality = lower volume)
- Respond to all replies within 24 hours

## DSAG Strategy (Priority)

DSAG events are your highest-leverage channel. 30,000+ members, mostly German-speaking, almost all genuine SAP decision-makers.

### Events to Target

- **DSAG Jahreskongress** (annual, usually October/November) — biggest stage. Submit a talk proposal 6 months in advance.
- **DSAG Technologietage** (annual, usually February) — more technical audience, perfect for our content.
- **Arbeitskreise** (working groups) — "Analytics und Big Data" and "SAP on Azure" meet several times a year. Join as a member; present informally.

### Local Meetups

- Frankfurt SAP User Group meetups
- Darmstadt Tech Meetup (broader, SAP-adjacent)
- SAPUI5 Entwickler meetups (developer audience)

## The Voice

Your LinkedIn voice is:

- Direct, not hype-y
- Confident in SAP expertise
- Genuinely interested in helping
- Not trying to sell Studio until Academy has proven value

Avoid:
- "Excited to announce"
- "I'm passionate about data"
- "Thoughts?" as a throwaway CTA
- Generic engagement bait

Good example of tone:
> "Published the expert ACDOCA walkthrough this morning. 24 steps, every one cited to SAP Help. If you're staring at a 2-billion-row Universal Journal extraction and wondering where to start, this is where. [link]"

Bad example:
> "🚀 Excited to share my latest passion project! The SAP Extract Academy is LIVE and I can't wait to hear what you think! Drop a 🔥 in the comments if you're ready to transform your data extraction! 💪"

The second one is cringe because it's generic. Yours is specific and confident.
