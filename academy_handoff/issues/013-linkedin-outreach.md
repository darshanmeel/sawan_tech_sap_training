# ISSUE-013: LinkedIn Outreach Setup and Campaign Launch

**Phase:** Launch (runs parallel to ISSUE-011)
**Estimated Effort:** 6-8 hours initial setup; ongoing 30-60 min/day
**Depends on:** ISSUE-011 (needs site live before outreach)
**Skill files:** `LINKEDIN_OUTREACH.md`

---

## Goal

Operationalize the LinkedIn outreach plan. Get from "we have a plan" to "we're sending messages daily" in one week.

---

## Part A: Profile Audit (Day 1)

Follow the checklist in `LINKEDIN_OUTREACH.md` → "Phase 1: Foundation":

- [ ] LinkedIn headline updated
- [ ] About section rewritten with Academy + SME positioning
- [ ] Featured section includes Academy landing page URL
- [ ] Banner image (1584×396) created — can be simple: dark background + "SAP Extract Academy" wordmark + Academy URL
- [ ] Profile photo is professional (not required but advised)

---

## Part B: Content Warm-Up (Weeks 1-4 Before Outreach)

Post 2-3×/week for 3-4 weeks BEFORE sending cold outreach. This builds signal for recipients that you're an active, credible voice in the space.

Content calendar template per `LINKEDIN_OUTREACH.md`:

- **Mondays — Teaching** (specific SAP concept)
- **Wednesdays — Observation** (field note)
- **Fridays — Community** (link to DSAG, SAP Community, reshare with comment)

Pre-write 8-12 posts in a draft doc. Publish on schedule.

---

## Part C: CRM Setup (Day 2)

Create a simple tracking spreadsheet or Hubspot free-tier setup with columns:

- Name
- Title
- Company
- Company Tier (SI Tier 1 / End Customer / Cloud Vendor / DSAG)
- Connection Sent Date
- Connection Accepted Date
- First Message Sent Date
- Reply Received
- Call Booked
- Pilot / Deal Status
- Notes

---

## Part D: Target List Building (Day 3-4)

Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator (€80/month) OR manual LinkedIn search.

Build three lists:

### List 1: SI Practice Leads — 50 targets

Filter:
- Company: Deloitte, Accenture, TCS, Infosys, Capgemini, PwC, Atos, LTIMindtree, Cognizant, HCL, Wipro, IBM Consulting, DXC, NTT Data, Syntax, Delaware, Apsolut
- Title contains: "SAP Data," "SAP Analytics," "SAP Practice," "S/4HANA Lead," "Head of SAP"
- Country: prioritize DACH region (Germany, Austria, Switzerland), then broader EU, then US
- Seniority: Senior Manager or above

### List 2: End Customer Data Leads — 100 targets

Filter:
- Industry: Manufacturing, Pharma, Retail, Energy, CPG, Finance
- Company size: 1000+ employees
- Title contains: "Head of Data," "Director Data Engineering," "VP Analytics," "Chief Data Officer"
- Country: prioritize Germany first (your local advantage), then DACH, then EU
- Additional filter: company known to run SAP (check company tech stack pages, LinkedIn company pages for SAP mentions)

### List 3: Cloud Vendor Partner Leads — 20 targets

Filter:
- Company: Microsoft, Snowflake, Databricks, Confluent, AWS, Google Cloud
- Title: "SAP Partner," "SAP GTM," "SAP Solutions Architect"
- Country: prioritize based on partner HQ geography (Microsoft in Germany is huge — target them first)

### List 4: DSAG Members — 30 targets

Specific to Germany:
- Filter by "DSAG" in About or experience
- Frankfurt/Hesse-based members get priority
- Mix of end customers, SIs, and SAP employees

---

## Part E: Connection Request Campaign (Week 5)

Using templates from `LINKEDIN_OUTREACH.md`:

- **Day 1:** 15 connection requests (mix of Tier 1 SIs and DSAG members — your highest-value segments)
- **Day 2:** 15 connection requests (end customers)
- **Day 3:** 10 connection requests (cloud vendors)
- **Day 4:** 15 more (rotate tiers)
- **Day 5:** 15 more (rotate tiers)

Keep at 15-20/day maximum. Higher volume triggers LinkedIn throttling and reduces acceptance rate.

Log every request in the CRM.

---

## Part F: First Messages (Week 5-6)

When connection is accepted, send first message within 2-3 days (not same day — looks spammy; not after a week — momentum lost).

Use the template matching their tier. Personalize one sentence per message:
- Reference a recent post they made
- Mention their company's stack from their About section
- Note a mutual connection if relevant

NEVER send a pure copy-paste template. LinkedIn users recognize it instantly and it destroys credibility.

---

## Part G: Follow-Up Cadence

If no reply within 2 weeks:

- First follow-up (2 weeks after first message): softer, different angle
- Second follow-up (4 weeks after first): "breakup" message — "I'll stop reaching out, but if ever useful, here's the link"
- No third follow-up

Update CRM with status after each interaction.

---

## Part H: Discovery Call Process

When someone replies with interest:

1. Book a 20-minute call (use Calendly/Cal.com for scheduling)
2. Send a calendar invite with a Google Meet or Teams link
3. Prepare a one-pager of Academy + Studio value props (1-page PDF or LinkedIn post pinned to feed)
4. During call:
   - First 5 min: understand their SAP data context (what tables, what cloud, what problem)
   - Middle 10 min: demo the Academy live, show ACDOCA expert walkthrough specifically
   - Last 5 min: ask about pilot interest, next steps

5. Send follow-up email within 24 hours:
   - Thanks + recap of their context
   - Specific Academy links relevant to their situation
   - Clear next step (pilot proposal, follow-up call in 2 weeks, etc.)

---

## Part I: Weekly Metrics Review

Every Friday, update the CRM and review:

- Connections sent / accepted this week
- Messages sent / replied
- Calls booked / held
- Pipeline progression (who moved stages)
- Learnings: what messages worked, what didn't

Adjust templates based on reply rate data. A/B test wording changes.
